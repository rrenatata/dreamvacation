Berlin

Known as the capital of Germany, Berlin is one of the most intriguing cities in Europe. The city lies on the Spree River which cuts through the center of the Berlin. The city is located approximately 112 miles south from the Baltic Sea, thus making the city's climate a bit cold. With its notorious history-specifically during the 20th century-Berlin continues to be one of the most influential cities. Pieces of Berlin's history still remain including parts of what was once the Berlin Wall. Culture is also a predominant aspect of this city. From art to literature and to even science, Berlin has made a significant contribution to the world. In fact, Albert Einstein lived in Berlin for approximately 18 years.

Berlin also has a wide variety of architecture. From incredibly modern and contemporary to historic and-odd to say-but Nazi architecture that was left after World War II, Berlin ceases to never get boring. The Fernsehturm (The Television Tower) and the Brandenburg Gate are probably the most well-known buildings in the city. These two buildings demonstrate the array of diverse architecture within Berlin. With the Brandenburg Gate being an 18th century arc that is one of the most recognizable landmarks in Europe, and The Fernsehturm, built as a reminder of the Sputnik Satellites and socialism shows how the history of this city is incorporated within every building.
